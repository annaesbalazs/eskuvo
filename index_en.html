We are very happy to have you on our wedding day!

We're getting married on Saturday afternoon, 16 September 2023 at the summer school camp of Szentjakabfa.
We'd be very glad if you could celebrate with us!

Venue

Balaton-felvidéki Erdészeti Erdei Iskola, Szentjakabfa
Both the ceremony and the garden party afterwards is taking place outside at the summer school.

As the location itself is quite rural we suggest you to arrive in comfortable, flat shoes or bring something comfy if you would like to get changed.

How to get there?

The summer school is in the middle of a forest in between Vigántpetend and Nagyvázsony, so the easiest way to get there is by car.
Arriving from Nagyvázsony on route 77, follow route 7311 and then take a left turn following the sign to a forestry exploration trail (expect around 2 km of dirt road!).
For those arriving by bus we're happy to take you from Veszprém or Nagyvázsony to make you journey easier. Please let us know this well in advance, so we can find a voluntary chauffeur.

Accommodation

At the venue there are rooms with 4-6 beds to rent, which are available from Friday.
There are 3 separate building with 34, 8 and 20 beds (either single beds or bunk beds).
You can also camp if you prefer that.

For those sleeping at another accommodation, there will be a driver to give you a ride during the night

Cost of accomodation

In rooms

Adult - 6000 HUF/person/night + 400 HUF/person/night taxes
Child (3-18 years old) - 4000 HUF/person/night
Child under 3 - free

Bed linen

The accommodation can provide you with a duvet and a pillow, but you need to bring bed linen (bedsheet, cover for duvet and pillow) or pay an extra fee of 1500 HUF/person.
Using sleeping bags is unfortunately not possible.

Good to know

As you can also see in the program, instead of having a traditional and elegant dinner we would like to have a free and easy-going potluck dinner, but we need your help to make that possible.
So please keep in mind the following things when coming to our wedding!

What not to expect:

  • - multiple course dishes like in restaurants
  • - waiters, waitresses and bartenders
  • - new glass for each of your drink
  • - staff to wash your glasses
  • - midnight meal

What to expect:

We bring
  • - multiple types of beer
  • - white and rose wine
  • - pálinka
  • - champagne for celebration
  • - wedding cake

Please bring
Food to share, something that you like to prepare in an amount that is enough for you.
This way we can all taste a variety of tasty things.
If you don't want to cook or bake anything, that's completely fine, just bring something else instead: soft drinks, shots or some food that you bought.
  Inspiration for the potluck dinner
To collect all the things that you bring here is a link to an excel sheet.
  What to pack?
Please be
  • - friendly, nice and helpful, look after yourselves and others too
  • - mindful and responsible, when we run out of glasses you may go and wash one for yourself in the common area.

What to pack?

- Pretty clothes for the ceremony

- Comfortable clothes if you'd like to change afterwards

- Blanket/sweater for the evening

- Comfortable shoes to dance on grass as there's no pavement or dance floor

- Bed linen or tent if you're sleeping in the camp

- Food or drink for potluck dinner

- Note for your food to know what it is and what's in it (with meat, vegan, if it contains any allergens)

- Drinks if you wish to drink other than beer, wine or palinka

- Reusable plastic glass (if you have some at home)
